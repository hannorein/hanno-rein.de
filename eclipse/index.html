April 8th 2024 Solar Eclipse from Toronto/GTA

A solar eclipse will be visible on April 8th 2024 from North America. This website provides information on how to best view the eclipse and is intended for people in Toronto or the Greater Toronto Area (GTA).

Background image credit: Rick Fienberg / TravelQuest International / Wilderness Travel
A map of the eclipse shadow over Toronto.

The total eclipse is NOT visible from Toronto

Unfortunately the total eclipse is not visible from Toronto. If you are in Toronto on April 8th, you will only see a partial solar eclipse. A partial solar eclipse is still an exciting event. However, it is very different from seeing a total eclipse. Even 1% of sunlight is enough to prevent you from seeing prominences in the sun's corona and stars in the sky. The map shows you the path of the northern line of the total eclipse over Toronto.

Where to go to see the total eclipse

If you live in Toronto and want to see the total eclipse, you need to travel. Coming from Toronto and travelling south-west, Burlington is the first city where you can see the total eclipse for a few seconds. The further you travel towards the central line of the total eclipse, the longer you will be able to experience totality. Buffalo is almost exactly on the central line and totality will last for about 4 minutes.

Coming from Toronto and travelling east, the first town where you can see the total eclipse for a few seconds is Cobourg. In the Kingston area, you can see the total eclipse for about 2 minutes. If you make it across the border and are in Watertown NY, then you will experience totality for about 4 minutes.

Timing

The eclipse will reach its maximum in Toronto at 3:20pm local time. This map shows the path of the total eclipse over Lake Erie and Lake Ontario. The maximum anywhere on this map ocurs between 3:15pm and 3:25pm.

Most of the path shown here is reachable withing a few hours drive from Toronto. But keep in mind that you are probably not the only one who is driving towards the central line of the eclipse on April 8th. Plan accordingly, especially if your trip includes a border crossing.

Eclipse glasses

If you are in Toronto on April 8th, never look directly at the sun! Because it will not be a total solar eclipse in Toronto, you need eclipse glasses to safely look at the sun at all times. Sunglasses, even if they are very dark, or other home made filters are not safe. If you travel to an area that will experience a total eclipse, then you can safely look at the sun for the few seconds/minutes that it is completely covered by the moon. It will be about as bright as a full moon.

Eclipse glasses are available for free from Toronto Public Libraries. If you are a student, staff, or faculty member at UTSC, then get one from the UTSC Library or directly from me (Thursdays, 3:30pm - 4:30pm, SW504C). You can also purchase glasses from various distributors. Make sure to not get ripped ripped off! Everything more than $3 or $4 is a scam!

Bad news: clouds!

Alright, so you have made it out to a place where you should see the total eclipse. I have some bad news for you: clouds! The map shows the average cloud cover in our area in April around the time of the eclipse. There is a 60% - 70% chance that there will be clouds. If there are clouds, it will still get very dark. However, you will not see the corona during totality.
